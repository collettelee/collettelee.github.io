IBM AI Governance

Animated video detailing the AI governance capabilities of IBM Cloud Pak for Data. This video is intended for onboarding new team members and educating potential clients. The project combines 2D and 3D animation to convey important information about the product in a fresh and exciting way, while staying true to IBM's established visual identity. Designed, illustrated, and animated this project as a Visual Design Intern (Data and AI) for IBM Cloud and Cognitive Software. The biggest challenges of this project were learning the isometric illustration style and 3D modeling the laptop mockup from scratch.

Script by Thomas Hollifield, voice over by Nicole Jones.

UCLA Arts Summer Institute Playing Cards

Special edition 54-card playing deck to be gifted to high school students attending the sold-out UCLA Arts Summer Institute program. I developed and illustrated a summery, playful, and colorful visual style that appeals to teenage consumers, along with face cards that stray from gender norms. The designs utilize simple, “imperfect” cut-out shapes to emulate scrapbooks one would make at summer camp. It was most important to the client that the cards be gender-neutral, meaning the team and I had to come up with new face cards to replace the King, Queen, Jack, and Joker hierarchy. We settled on Sun, Moon, Star, and Blackhole, calling back to the nature exploration aspect of summer camp. The client asked that the suits be unique to the UCLA Arts identity, so I devised a system where each department of the school is abstractly represented by a simple, recognizable shape and color scheme. These symbols allow for straightforward gameplay through the use of easy and familiar shapes, while also tying in the values of each department.

The cards were such a big hit among UCLA admin that the original order of 500 decks was doubled to 1000 for distribution across all departments. Additionally, we repurposed the design for exclusive t-shirts to gift to the student body at the 2023-2024 UCLA Arts Opening Reception.

Tomino's Hell

Augmented reality poster experimenting with typography, 3D animation, and sound design. Based on the cursed Japanese poem Tomino's Hell and designed in collaboration with Kathleen Yang and Sabrina Chang. Tomino's Hell is a notorious Japanese urban legend about a boy named Tomino and his journey through hell. It is said that reading the poem out loud will result in death. The animated glass beads, winding blood stream, and foggy forest background are all references to lines in the poem. The Japanese texts are phrases from the poem. The dashed boxes around certain phrases are a visual reference to Genkouyoushi paper, which are usually used by Japanese students to practice writing Kanji.

The Rise of Bread

Two 17"x22" informative posters diving deep into wheat, corn, and rice and each grain's breadmaking process. Researched, designed, and baked in collaboration with Peyton Gee and Valentin Nguyen. We illustrated the overlap in ingredients between wheat bread, cornbread, and rice bread using a Venn diagram. At the suggestion of media artist and UCLA Art|Sci Center director Victoria Vesna, the ingredients were displayed in petri dishes to create a sense of visual harmony with the circular forms of the diagram.

Egg Lamp

A lamp product meant to look like a beautiful mistake, no matter what surface it is placed on. This piece of home decor is an oversized, accidentally cracked raw egg containing a glowing LED light in its yolk to illuminate any space. Through its clear egg white, environmental texture becomes a part of the work itself. Working prototype was made with laser-cut acrylic material and half of a ball pit ball. Selected for the 2023 DMA Undergraduate Exhibition at UCLA.
